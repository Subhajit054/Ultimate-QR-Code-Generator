How to Create a QR Code Online — Step-by-step (Free)

Easy guide • Generate URL, text, phone & WiFi QR • Download PNG / SVG

QR codes (Quick Response codes) are a fast way to share links, contact info, WiFi details, or short text with any smartphone. In this simple guide you’ll learn how to create a QR code online in under a minute and download it as a high-quality PNG or SVG — using the free QR Maker tool.

1. Choose the type of QR code

Decide what information your QR will contain: a website URL, plain text, a phone number, an email, or WiFi credentials. Most tools let you switch between types. For a URL, paste the complete address (including “https://”). For WiFi, fill in SSID|WPA|password so phones can connect via a scan.

2. Enter the data

Type or paste the content into the input field. For an email QR the format used by many generators is email|subject|body. For phone QR, use an international format (e.g. +911234567890) to ensure compatibility across countries.

3. Customize size & colors

Choose a size (300–1200 px). Larger sizes give cleaner results when printing. Select a foreground color and background color. Keep a good contrast (dark foreground on light background) so QR scanners can read the code easily.

4. Add a logo (optional)

Many makers allow a small logo in the center. If you add a logo, use a high-contrast image and don’t cover too much of the QR area — 10–20% is safe. The generator uses error-correction so small logos still allow scanning.

5. Generate & test

Click Generate. Always test the QR with 2–3 different phones (Android & iPhone) and apps. Open the scanned link and check that WiFi or email actions work as expected.

6. Download as PNG or SVG

Download PNG for images and social sharing. Download SVG for printing or editing in vector software — SVG preserves crisp edges at any size. Keep a copy of both if you plan to reuse the design.

Best practices & tips

  • Keep the QR size big enough for the use-case: business cards ≈ 2cm, posters ≈ 10cm+.
  • Use a short URL (or URL shortener) to reduce characters and improve scan reliability.
  • Add a short call-to-action near the QR, for example “Scan to visit our menu”.
  • Test printed QR codes after printing — colors and paper can affect scanning.
