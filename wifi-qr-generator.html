WiFi QR Code Generator — Let Guests Connect Instantly

Create a WiFi QR code (WPA/WPA2) — share with guests so they can connect with one scan.

Sharing your WiFi details manually is tedious. A WiFi QR code allows anyone to scan and join your network without typing SSID or password. This page explains how to make a WiFi QR correctly and what format to use for maximum compatibility.

WiFi QR format

Most QR tools follow the standard format: WIFI:T:WPA;S:SSID;P:password;;. Example: WIFI:T:WPA;S:MyNetwork;P:secretpass;;. If your network has no password (open WiFi), use WIFI:T:nopass;S:MyNetwork;;.

Step-by-step: create your WiFi QR

  1. Enter SSID — The exact network name (case sensitive).
  2. Select Encryption — Usually WPA (WPA/WPA2). Choose WEP only for very old routers.
  3. Enter password — Type the WiFi password exactly (no extra spaces).
  4. Generate — Click the generate button and test the QR with a phone.
  5. Test & print — Test with Android & iPhone; then print or display the QR for guests.

Compatibility tips

Most modern phones interpret the WiFi QR and offer to connect automatically. iPhones and Androids handle the standard format well. If a device does not auto-join, scanning apps will typically show the SSID and password so the user can connect manually.

Where to place the WiFi QR

Put it in guest rooms, reception desks, cafes, or on a shelf near the entrance. Consider printing a small card (2–4 in) with a short instruction like “Scan to connect to WiFi”.

Security & privacy

Only share WiFi QR codes with trusted guests. If you post your WiFi QR online (e.g., for a public event), make sure you use a guest network with limited access to sensitive resources.
